HERITAGE LOCATION

GALLE FORT

The old town of Galle

On the shores of the Indian Ocean,100km south of Colombo situated The city of Galle in the southwest of Sri Lanka .Galle port houses a natural port because it is built on a rocky peninsula and accessing this port can be complicated because of the massive coral reefs surrounding.

Urban Morphology

The peninsula can be seen to north to south .Galle had a appearance of a fortified stronghold since the 18TH century.at the city’s north end there can be found bastions integrated walls and a drawbridge which protects the fortified fort .Other building can be seen of military character .near port some Commercial and storage buildings can also be found .The dwellings with their dressed stone walls and large doors built by the Dutch embellish Galle’s quite streets to this day.

Historical Reference

Since ancient times Galle bay has been used. The oldest commercial ports of the Levant is protected by the Galle bay. It is stated that Galle bay was the most active port in Sri Lanka in the 16Th century. After Portuguese settled on the island in 1505, they built a trading post in Galle in 1507 and established the small fort of Santa Cruz. At the north end of peninsula rampart was erected soon after they returned from Colombo. As well as they added three bastions to this rampart when the island was threatened by the Dutch in 1625.
