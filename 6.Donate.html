Yaka masks


Sri Lanka has inherited and been influenced by mask making (vesmuhunu; ) and devil dance traditions mostly from the Indian towns of Kerala and Malabar, although Sri Lankan artisans have managed to include more decorative techniques and color in today's masks. When compared to the ones discovered in ancient Sri Lanka, the technique of mask manufacturing has been perfected over time, and craftsmen have been able to produce new and superior designs.




Tea


Tea is an aromatic beverage made by steeping cured or fresh leaves of Camellia sinensis, an evergreen shrub native to China, India, and other East Asian nations, in hot or boiling water. Camellia taliensis leaves are also infrequently used to make tea. It is, after water, the most extensively drank beverage on the planet.
